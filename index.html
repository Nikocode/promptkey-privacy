PromptKey

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 16, 2026

PromptKey is designed to keep dictation local to your Mac whenever possible. The app listens only while you actively start dictation, transcribes speech on-device, and inserts text into the apps you choose to use.

What PromptKey Processes

  • Microphone audio captured while you are dictating
  • Transcribed text created from that audio
  • The name of the app you are dictating into so text can be delivered to the right place
  • Optional local diagnostic logs and replay artifacts stored on your Mac
  • Your selected settings, such as push-to-talk key and overlay preferences

How PromptKey Uses Permissions

  • Microphone access so it can capture speech while dictation is active
  • Accessibility access so it can insert text into other apps more reliably
  • Input Monitoring so it can respond to a global push-to-talk key

PromptKey does not use these permissions for advertising, tracking, or background monitoring.

On-Device Processing

Speech recognition runs locally on your Mac using downloaded model files. PromptKey does not send your microphone audio or dictated text to a remote speech-to-text service for transcription.

Network Use

PromptKey may use the network to download model or tokenizer files the first time they are needed, or when you refresh a local model cache.

These downloads may come from:

  • Hugging Face
  • GitHub
  • OpenAI-hosted tokenizer files

These requests are used to fetch model assets and supporting files. They are not used to upload your dictated audio for transcription.

Local Storage

PromptKey may store data locally on your Mac, including:

  • Downloaded model files
  • App settings
  • Recent transcripts shown in the app
  • Structured logs
  • Optional replay artifacts if diagnostic capture is enabled

Clipboard fallback may temporarily place dictated text on your clipboard when PromptKey decides it is safer to copy instead of auto-pasting.

Sharing

PromptKey does not sell your data. PromptKey does not include third-party advertising SDKs or analytics SDKs in this build.

Your network provider and any model-hosting service you download files from may receive standard request metadata such as your IP address when asset downloads occur.

Your Control

  • Revoke permissions in macOS System Settings
  • Remove local app data by deleting the app's container and support files
  • Disable replay artifact capture in Developer Tools if you do not want diagnostic artifacts saved locally

Contact

For privacy questions about PromptKey, contact the developer through the support channel used for the build you received.

This page is intended to satisfy the public privacy policy requirement for PromptKey distribution and App Store metadata.
